Lava Lamp Experiment

The lava lamp experiment showcases the fascinating properties of liquids, specifically the interaction between oil and vinegar. In this demonstration, a glass cup is filled with baking soda and oil, creating a liquid mixture. In another cup, a small amount of vinegar is mixed with a chosen color. When the cup with oil is placed on top of an LED light source and the vinegar mixture is poured into it, a captivating chemical reaction occurs.

When vinegar is poured into oil, it reacts with baking soda, resulting in the formation of carbon dioxide gas. This gas creates bubbles that rise and carry the colored vinegar with them. Rising bubbles create the illusion of a volcanic eruption, and the colored liquid resembles lava. When the bubbles reach the top, they burst and deposit the colored liquid back into the oil, creating a mesmerizing, continuous motion cycle reminiscent of a lava lamp.

Materials

  • Jar
  • Vegetable Oil
  • Baking Soda
  • Vinegar
  • Food Coloring
